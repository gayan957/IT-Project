Trash2Cash.lk - System Knowledge Base

1. Core Concept and Goal

1.1. The Problem We Solve
In Sri Lanka, the informal waste collection industry lacks transparency, structure, and trust. Diesel trucks operate without schedules, use unregulated pricing, and provide no accountability, leaving customers unsure of the real value of their recyclables. This system leads to noise and air pollution, unsafe handling of hazardous materials, and inefficient recycling processes.

1.2. Our Solution: Trash2Cash.lk
Trash2Cash.lk is a web-based digital platform designed to formalize and modernize Sri Lanka's recyclable waste management. It connects households and businesses (Customers) with verified Pickup Agents and certified Recyclers through a structured, transparent, and efficient ecosystem.

1.3. Our Aim
Our goal is to create a "Smart Waste Collection Network" for Sri Lanka. This platform allows users to schedule pickups, track their contributions, and receive fair rewards, while ensuring that pickup agents operate efficiently and recyclers receive clean, sorted materials.

2. User Roles & Stakeholders

The platform is designed for five primary stakeholders:

- Customers (Households & Businesses): The generators of recyclable waste. They use the system to schedule pickups, monitor their smart bins, track earnings, and ensure their waste is handled responsibly.
- Pickup Agents (Drivers/Collectors): The individuals who physically collect the waste from customers. The platform provides them with optimized routes, clear schedules, and a transparent way to track their earnings and commissions.
- Pickup Partners (Licensed Collectors): These are the companies or organizations that employ and manage the pickup agents. They use the platform as a centralized tool to manage their fleet, track collection volumes, handle complaints, and oversee revenue.
- Recyclers: The end-buyers of the collected materials. They use the platform to find and purchase a consistent, reliable supply of sorted, quality-certified recyclable waste.
- Administrators: The operators of the Trash2Cash.lk platform. They are responsible for managing user accounts, setting waste prices, monitoring system activity, and ensuring the smooth operation of the entire ecosystem.

3. Core System Modules & Features

The system is built around five major modules:

3.1. Bin Management
- Smart Bins: Registered users can be assigned an IoT-enabled smart bin. These bins are equipped with ultrasonic sensors to monitor waste levels in real-time.
- Automated Notifications: When a smart bin reaches its optimal capacity, it automatically sends a notification to both the user and the nearest available pickup agent. This optimizes pickup routes and prevents overflow.
- Manual Scheduling: Users without smart bins can still schedule pickups manually through the platform.
- Live Map View: Pickup agents can view a live map showing the locations of full or nearly-full bins in their assigned area.

3.2. Finance Management
- Transparent Payments: Customer payments are calculated automatically based on the weight and type of waste collected, using current market rates set by the admin.
- Digital Transactions: Payments are processed through integrated digital wallets or direct bank transfers (using the Stripe API). Customers receive transaction confirmations and can view a detailed history.
- Automated Commissions: The system automatically calculates and deposits commissions for pickup agents for each successful collection.
- Financial Reporting: The platform generates comprehensive financial reports, including transaction summaries, revenue reports, and agent commission statements for administrators and partners.

3.3. Pickup Agent & Waste Management (For Partners)
- Agent Management: Pickup Partners can register new agents, assign them unique IDs, and define daily collection routes.
- Performance Tracking: The system monitors key agent metrics like the number of pickups, average weight collected, customer satisfaction ratings, and on-time performance.
- Complaint Resolution: Customer complaints about agents are routed directly to the responsible Pickup Partner for review and resolution.

3.4. Waste Management (For Recyclers)
- Inventory Dashboard: Recyclers can view a real-time inventory of available recyclable materials from various pickup partners, with details on waste type, quantity, quality grade, and location.
- Structured Ordering: Recyclers can place bulk orders for specific materials directly through the platform. The pickup partner receives the request for approval, ensuring a reliable and efficient supply chain.

3.5. HR & System Management (For Admins)
- User & Role Management: Admins have centralized control to create, modify, and manage all user accounts and roles (customers, agents, partners, recyclers).
- Pricing Control: Admins can set and update the market rates for different categories of waste based on current commodity prices.
- System Configuration: Admins can customize platform-wide settings, including collection policies and quality standards.

4. Technical Specifications

Architecture: MERN Stack
- Frontend: React.js for a responsive, dynamic, and user-friendly interface.
- Backend: Node.js and Express.js to build a robust RESTful API that handles all business logic.
- Database: MongoDB (NoSQL) for flexible storage of user profiles, pickup records, IoT data, and more.

Authentication: JWT (JSON Web Tokens) are used for secure session management.

IoT Integration:
- Microcontroller: ESP32 modules are installed in smart bins for Wi-Fi enabled data transmission.
- Sensors: Ultrasonic sensors for fill-level detection.

API Integrations:
- Payment Gateway: Stripe API for secure digital payment processing.
- Maps & Routing: Google Maps API for route optimization and live map displays.

Development Methodology: Agile Software Engineering. The project was developed in iterative sprints to ensure flexibility, collaboration, and continuous feedback.
